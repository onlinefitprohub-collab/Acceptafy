You can turn ESP API access into a *monster* “early warning + diagnosis” engine – not just dashboards.

At a high level:

> **Pull raw events + campaign metadata from ESPs → normalize it → build insight layers that predict and *prevent* bad placement (spam/Promotions) before and after sends.**

1. **What data you can pull from ESP APIs**

---

## 1. What ESP APIs actually give you (raw ingredients)

Popular ESPs like SendGrid and Mailgun expose very similar event streams via APIs/webhooks:

* **Delivery events:** processed, delivered, deferred, dropped, bounced([Twilio][1])
* **Engagement events:** opens, clicks, unsubscribes, spam complaints([Twilio][1])
* **Suppression list data:** bounces, unsubscribes, spam complaints stored long-term([Campaign Refinery][2])
* **Metadata:** campaign IDs/names, categories/tags, timestamps, IPs, user-agent, URL clicked, etc.([Twilio][1])

You can also usually fetch:

* **Campaign definitions:** subject, from address, sending domain, list/segment used
* **Per-domain stats:** opens/clicks/bounces/complaints by recipient domain (gmail.com, outlook.com, yahoo.com, etc.)
* **Tracking settings:** open/click tracking on/off per domain/campaign([documentation.mailgun.com][3])

That’s enough to build some **very smart “don’t go to spam” brains**.

## 2. Insight engines you can build from that data

### A. Early-warning deliverability radar (by mailbox provider)

**Use:** delivery + bounce + deferral + complaint events segmented by recipient domain
**Build:**

* **Provider health panels:**

  * “Gmail: bounces 0.7%, deferrals 1.2%, complaints 0.05% (rising)”
  * “Microsoft: deferrals spiked on last 2 campaigns”
* **Trend detection:**

  * Detect when Gmail metrics diverge from Outlook/Yahoo – classic sign of provider-specific filtering.
* **Smart alerts:**

  * “Your Gmail deferral rate is 3× your 30-day baseline – likely soft spam filtering or throttling.”
  * “Microsoft complaints exceeded 0.3% on this campaign – high risk of junk folder next time.”

👉 **Impact:** catches issues *days* before the sender notices “all my opens died.”

---

### B. Campaign “black box recorder” – what went wrong where

Tie **events + metadata** per campaign:

* Build a **campaign forensics view**:

  * Delivery funnel (processed → delivered → opened → clicked) per provider.
  * Bounce breakdown: unknown user vs blocked vs policy-related.
  * Complaint + unsubscribe spikes mapped to **specific links, content blocks, or segments** (using categories/tags + URL-level click data).
* Show **“last good campaign vs bad one”**:

  * What changed? Subject line, from address, link domain, list segment, send time, volume jump.

Feature examples:

* “This campaign had 4× your usual Gmail complaint rate and 2× unsubscribes; the only change was a new landing page domain + more aggressive subject line.”
* “Compared to your median campaign, this one hit a 40% increase in deferrals at Outlook immediately after a 2× volume increase.”

👉 **Impact:** you stop people repeating the same mistake that caused the last spam incident.

---

### C. List health & segment risk scoring (contact-level “should we send?”)

From events + suppressions you can build **list and segment health scores**:

* **Per-contact health:**

  * last open/click date by provider
  * history of soft bounces
  * “never engaged” streaks
* **Per-segment risk:**

  * complaint/unsubscribe trend per segment
  * unknown-user bounce rate (signals list quality/purchased lists)

Features:

* “Don’t send” recommendations:

  * “This segment has 0 engagement in 180 days at Gmail and a 2% unknown-user bounce rate – auto-suppress or run re-permission campaign.”
* **Smart sunsetting rules**, driven by real data:

  * Different thresholds by provider (e.g. Gmail is stricter than some corporate MXs).
* **Acquisition source scoring:**

  * Tag contacts by signup source (lead magnet, list import, webinar, partner).
  * Show which sources produce the most complaints/bounces at which providers.

👉 **Impact:** you cut out the toxic parts of the list that pull everyone into spam together.

---

### D. Cadence & fatigue insights (how often is “too often” per provider)

Using send timestamps + open/click/unsub/complain data:

* **Per-recipient fatigue curves:**

  * “When you email Jane@Gmail more than 4×/week, her probability of opening drops below 3% and odds of unsubscribe/complain increase.”
* **Per-segment frequency recommendations:**

  * Compare frequency vs complaint/unsub behaviour across campaigns.
* **“Frequency shock” detector:**

  * Detect sudden jumps in volume/frequency to a segment and measure impact on complaints/deferrals.

Features:

* “If you double your sending frequency to this segment, historically similar moves increased complaints 2.1× for Gmail – consider splitting or warming them.”

👉 **Impact:** avoids the “we just sent way too many promos and tanked our reputation” problem.

---

### E. Content + template risk scoring (without pretending you’re a spam filter)

You don’t see folders (Inbox vs Promotions vs Spam) via ESP APIs, but you can infer **risky content patterns** by correlating events with template and URL metadata.

Use:

* Template IDs / names
* Subject lines + preview text
* Link domains & redirect patterns
* Per-provider engagement & complaint metrics

You can build:

* **Template health scores:**

  * “Template A has 40% lower opens and 2× complaints at Gmail compared to your other templates.”
* **Link-domain risk:**

  * “Campaigns using trackingdomain.foo had 3× higher Gmail deferrals than those without.”
* **Promotion-like pattern detectors:**

  * Heavy promo words, lots of images, multiple CTAs, discount-heavy subject lines *combined with* lower Gmail open rates vs non-promo templates.

You can’t guarantee “Primary vs Promotions”, but you CAN say:

> “When you send this kind of template to this audience at Gmail, your open rate behaves like other known ‘Promotions-style’ sends and your long-term engagement trends down.”

👉 **Impact:** pushes users towards safer templates, URLs, and structures *for their specific audience*.

---

### F. Multi-ESP & multi-stream consistency

If a customer uses several ESPs or different streams (transactional vs marketing):

* Normalize events from all ESPs into one model.
* Show **cross-stream interactions**:

  * “When marketing blasts go out right after failed transactional sends (bounced invoices / password resets), complaint rates spike.”
* Show **ESP-specific behaviour**:

  * “Your campaigns on ESP A are fine at Gmail but struggling at Outlook; ESP B is fine everywhere. Possible IP/domain reputation issues.”

👉 **Impact:** tells them whether the problem is:
“our list/content/cadence” vs “this provider’s infra/ IP pool is under a cloud.”

---

### G. Benchmarks & predictive risk models

Once you have enough volume:

* Build **internal benchmarks** per industry/volume band/provider.
* Train simple models that estimate:

  * Expected complaint rate given list age, segment, content type, volume, provider mix.
  * Expected open rate drop at Gmail vs global average (a proxy for Promotions/spam drift).

Productized as:

* **Pre-send “risk badge” on every campaign:**

  * GREEN: metrics in line with historical safe sends.
  * AMBER: “Frequency + list age makes this riskier than 80% of your previous sends.”
  * RED: “Similar sends in the past led to high complaints at Gmail.”

👉 **Impact:** stops bad sends *before* they go out, acting like a “speed limiter” on reputation damage.

---

## 3. How this directly helps avoid spam/Promotions

Using ESP APIs, your app can:

1. **Spot trouble early by provider** (deferrals, complaints, bounces) instead of waiting for “my opens died.”
2. **Identify *which* campaigns, templates, domains, and segments are causing the problem.**
3. **Recommend surgical fixes** (suppress specific segments, back off specific providers, adjust frequency, change tracking/link domains) instead of vague “your deliverability is bad.”
4. **Guard every future send** with a pre-flight risk check powered by *their own* historical data + your benchmarks.
